### Unit 4 Hit it big, Wrap up & Let's explore: 1. Sending Messages in Ancient Times

Long ago, without phones or computers, people found clever ways to send messages.

Yanhuo (Smoke Signals)
Soldiers on the Great Wall used smoke to send signals. They created different amounts or types of smoke to mean different things. For example, one column of smoke often meant "Enemy is coming." This way was fast and could be seen from far away, but it could only send simple messages.

Feige (Messenger Pigeons)
People used homing pigeons to carry small notes. These pigeons had a great sense of direction and could fly long distances back to their home. This way was reliable for sending messages over great distances, but it needed special training for the birds.

Benyi (Running Messengers)
Important news was sent by runners who travelled from place to place. While these messengers could share complex information clearly, this way was much slower than the others.

Life was different in the past, but people were smart. They found ways to share information and stay connected.

Smoke Signals: Soldiers used different smoke to mean different things. It was fast and could be seen far away.
Messenger Pigeons: People used pigeons to carry small notes. It was reliable but needed training.
Running Messengers: Runners carried news from place to place. It was slow or slower but could tell complex things.

Question 1: What could one column of smoke mean on the Great Wall?
Answer: Enemy was coming.

Question 2: What is true about messenger pigeons?
Answer: They needed special training.

Question 3: What is the main idea of the passage?
Answer: Ancient People found clever ways to send messages.

Question 4: Which way would you like to use to send messages in ancient times?
Answer: I would like to use messenger pigeons because they are reliable.

### Unit 4 Hit it big, Wrap up & Let's explore: 2. Fun Games in Old Times

Long ago, when your grandparents were children, they didn't have phones or computers like we do today. But do you know what? They had so many fun games to play outside! Let me tell you about some interesting games from old times.

Cuju was one of the most popular games in ancient China. It was very similar to modern football. Players would kick a leather ball filled with feathers into a net. Both children and adults loved playing Cuju in open fields.

Touhu was another favourite game. Players would try to throw long, thin sticks into a narrow pot from a distance. This game was often played at parties and celebrations.

Children also enjoyed flying kites of different shapes and animals. They would run in open fields trying to make their kites fly high.

Hide-and-seek was always a favourite too! Children would hide behind trees, walls, or bushes while one person counted with eyes closed. Then the seeker would try to find all the hidden friends.

The best part was that all these games were played outside with friends. Children would meet after school and play until dinner time. They didn't need expensive toys but just their imagination and friends to play with!

Question 1: What was Cuju similar to?
Answer: Football.

Question 2: What did children need to play Touhu?
Answer: Pots and sticks.

Question 3: Where did children usually play these games?
Answer: Outside with friends.

Question 4: Which game do children play when some hide behind trees?
Answer: Hide-and-seek.

Question 5: What was the main difference between old games and modern games?
Answer: Old games were mostly played outside.

Question 6: Which game do you want to play?
Answer: I want to play Cuju because I am good at playing ball games.
