### Unit 6 Hit it big, Wrap up & Let's explore: 2. China's Four Great New Inventions

Today, China is known for new inventions. They are called the "Four Great New Inventions". High-speed Rail: China has the world's largest network of high-speed trains. These trains are fast, clean, and comfortable. They can take you to faraway cities in just a few hours. Mobile Payment: Nowadays, people don't use cash that much. They use QR codes to pay for almost everything. Apps like Alipay and WeChat Pay make this possible. Bike-sharing: Have you seen colourful bikes parked on the streets? They are shared bikes. You can unlock them with your phone, ride them to your destination, and then park them. It can cover the "last mile" of your trip and is good for the environment. E-commerce: Online shopping is very popular in China. Platforms like Taobao and JD sell millions of products. You can buy things from clothes to groceries online and get them delivered quickly, sometimes they arrive on the day you buy. These "Four Great New Inventions" make daily life in China much more convenient. They show how modern technology is changing the ways we live, travel, and shop.

Question 1: What is the main topic of this passage? Answer: Four New Great Inventions of China.
Question 2: Which invention uses QR codes to buy things? Answer: Mobile payment.
Question 3: According to the passage, why is bike-sharing good? Answer: Because it helps with the "last mile" problem and is good for the environment.
Question 4: Which invention allows you to buy things at home? Answer: E-commerce.
Question 5: What does the word "convenient" mean? Answer: Easy and fitting well with your needs.

### Unit 6 Formative Practice 6: 13. The Green Miracle of Saihanba

About sixty years ago, Saihanba in China was a large sandy place. There were very few trees. Strong winds often blew yellow sand everywhere. Because there were not enough plants, animals had no food or homes. It was hard for people to live there. In the 1960s, a group of hard-working people came with a big dream: to plant trees and turn this poor land into a green forest. The work was very difficult. Cold winter and dry, hot summer comes every year. There was little water, so they had to carry water from far away for the young trees. But they never gave up. Year after year, they kept planting trees. Slowly, the small trees began to grow. After about ten years, the land is dotted with green. After many years of hard work, something amazing happened-Saihanba became a big, healthy forest! Today, Saihanba is one of the largest man-made forests in the world. Millions of trees now grow on the once empty land. The air is clean, and many animals have come back to live there. This wonderful change teaches us that if we work together and never give up, we can do good things for our world.

Question 1: What was Saihanba like sixty years ago? Answer: A large sandy place with few trees.
Question 2: When did people start planting trees in Saihanba? Answer: In the 1960s.
Question 3: What was the weather like in Saihanba? Answer: Very cold in winter and hot in summer.
Question 4: How long did it take for green places to start showing? Answer: About ten years.
Question 5: What can we learn from this story? Answer: Working together and never giving up.
