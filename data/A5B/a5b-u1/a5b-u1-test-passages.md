### Unit 1 Get ready & Start up: Listening Dialogues

Jack: Hi, Lucy! How are you?
Lucy: I'm great, Jack! I was just thinking how much we've changed.
Jack: Me too. When I was five, I learned to swim.
Lucy: Cool! I learned to swim when I was seven. I was scared of the water at first, but my dad helped me.
Jack: That's amazing! Growing up is so much fun. There are always new things to try!
Lucy: I agree with you.

Question 1: What did Lucy learn to do when she was seven? Answer: Swam.
Question 2: Who thinks growing up is so much fun? Answer: Both of them.

Lily: Hi Tom, do you remember when we first met?
Tom: Sure, Lily! In Grade 1, you helped me find my lost pencil case.
Lily: Yeah! Now we're in Grade 5. Time flies when we're together.
Tom: We've shared so much. When I failed the math test, you taught me every day. You always shared your learning methods with me.
Lily: English was hard for me, and you always learned with me together. Friends make hard days easier.
Tom: I hope we'll be friends forever, even when we grow up.
Lily: Me too!

Question 3: When did they first meet? Answer: In Grade 1.
Question 4: Which one is NOT true? Answer: It was hard for them to be friends.
Question 5: What is the dialogue mainly about? Answer: It's great to grow up with friends.

### Unit 1 Fuel up: Reading Passage 2 (Plant Growth)



Today is March 15th. I put some flower seeds into a big pot. I put in some water, too. Now it's in the sun. No sprout yet. Today is April 1st. I am so excited. I can see a sprout. It's small and green. It's so lovely. I water it again and put it in the sun. Today is April 15th. My plant has two green leaves. I water it every day and make sure it gets lots of sunlight. Today is May 1st. My plant is one month old. I keep a close watch on it every day. It's tall and strong, but it has no flowers. Oh, come on! I can hardly wait.

Question 1: Plant's State on 15th April. Answer: Has two green leaves.

Question 3: When did the writer first water the flower seeds? Answer: The writer first watered the flower seeds on March 15th.
Question 4: What did the plant look like on April 1st? Answer: On April 1st the plant had a small and green sprout.

### Unit 1 Hit it big, Wrap up & Let's explore: Reading Passage 2 (Needs and Wants in Growing up)



Liam is a primary school student. He got $10 for his 10th birthday and felt so excited. He wanted a new video game, but his old shoes had holes. "Which to choose?" he wondered. His mum said, "Needs are things we must have-like shoes to keep your feet safe. Wants are things that make us happy, but we can live without them." Liam bought new shoes. He felt cozy, and he didn't trip anymore. Later, he saved pocket money and got the game. His mum smiled, "Growth is learning to tell needs from wants. You can have both just not always at the same time." Liam nodded. Now he checks, "Do I need this or just want it?" It helps him make smart choices.

Question 1: What did Liam receive for his 10th birthday? Answer: Some money.
Question 2: What does "cozy" mean in the passage? Answer: Comfortable.
Question 3: According to Liam's mum, what is a "need"? Answer: Something we must have.
Question 4: What happened after Liam bought new shoes? Answer: He saved money and later got the game.
Question 5: What does the story mainly talk about? Answer: It's important to tell needs from wants in growing up.

### Unit 1 Formative Practice: Reading Passage (Confucius and the Young Carpenter)



Long ago, a 10-year-old boy named Kong Hui loved making wooden toys. One day, he met Confucius, a wise man. Kong Hui showed Confucius a small wooden cart he made, but it couldn't roll straight. He felt sad and wanted to give up. Confucius smiled and said, "Crafts, like virtues, need patience. Look at bamboo-it grows slowly but stands tall." The boy listened. He checked the cart's wheels again, fixed the uneven parts, and tried again. After many tries, the cart rolled smoothly! Confucius patted his head, "You didn't just fix a cart-you learned to persist, never give up." From that day, Kong Hui kept practicing. Years later, he became a great carpenter. He always remembered Confucius' words: True growth comes from trying hard, just like our ancestors taught us.

Question 1: What did Kong Hui love to do when he was 10? Answer: He loved making wooden toys.
Question 2: Why did Kong Hui feel sad when showing his wooden cart to Confucius? Answer: His wooden cart couldn't roll straight.
Question 3: What did Kong Hui do after listening to Confucius's words? Answer: He checked the wheels and fixed the uneven parts.
Question 4: What did Kong Hui learn by fixing the cart? Answer: The virtue of persistence and never giving up.
Question 5: Which one is true about the passage? Answer: True growth comes from trying hard.
