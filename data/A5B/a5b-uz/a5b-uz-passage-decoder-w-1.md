### Section 13 Reading Comprehension: 13. Libraries to Go

Do you often go to libraries? What is the library like in your city? In fact, there are many different libraries around the world.
Camel Library
In small villages in Kenya, Africa, many kids love to read. But there are no roads, only sand. Cars and trucks cannot go there, so camels carry library books to the children. Two camels carry books and a small tent. The librarian sets up the tent and lets kids read quietly. New books come to the villages every two weeks.
Elephant Library
In the mountains of Thailand, elephants carry books in strong metal boxes. The boxes can keep books safe from heavy rain and the wet weather there.
Boat Library
Indonesia has many islands. People travel by water, so library boats stop at small villages and leave new books for the people there.
The most important part of these special libraries is bringing people not only books, but also joy and hope.
