# Unit 9 Present perfect continuous (**I have been doing**)

### A
**It's been raining.**
Study this example situation:

[*VISUAL: Illustration of a red car parked on a wet street with puddles. The sky is grey, but it is not currently raining.*]

Is it raining?
No, but the ground is wet.
**It's been raining.** (= It **has** been ...)

**have/has been** + **-ing** is the *present perfect continuous*:

| | | | |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I/we/they/you<br>he/she/it | **have** (= I've etc.)<br>**has** (= he's etc.) | **been** | **doing**<br>**working**<br>**learning** etc. |

We use the present perfect continuous for an activity that has recently stopped or just stopped:
* Why are you out of breath? **Have** you **been running**?
* Paul is very tired. He**'s been working** hard.
* Why are you so tired? What **have** you **been doing**?
* I**'ve been talking** to Amanda and she agrees with me.
* Where have you been? I**'ve been looking** for you.

[*LAYOUT: A right-pointing yellow arrow labeled "**have/has been + -ing** present perfect continuous", ending exactly at a vertical line labeled "now".*]

### B
**It's been raining for two hours.**
Study this example situation:

[*VISUAL: Illustration of a person wearing a yellow raincoat and holding a green and blue umbrella, standing on a sidewalk in the pouring rain next to a red postbox.*]

It began raining two hours ago and it is still raining.
How long **has** it **been raining**?
It**'s been raining** for two hours. (= It **has** been ...)

We use the present perfect continuous in this way, especially with **how long**, **for** ... and **since** ... . The activity is still happening (as in this example) or has just stopped.

* **How long have** you **been learning** English? (= you're still learning English)
* Ben is watching TV. He**'s been watching** TV **all day**.
* Where have you been? I**'ve been looking** for you **for the last half hour**.
* Chris **hasn't been feeling** well **recently**.

You can use the present perfect continuous for repeated actions:
* Silvia is a very good tennis player. She**'s been playing since she was eight**.
* Every morning they meet in the same cafe. They**'ve been going** there **for years**.

### C
Compare **I am doing** and **I have been doing**:

[*LAYOUT: Two-column comparison.*]

**Column 1:**
> **I am doing**
> *present continuous*
>
> [*LAYOUT: A vertical line labeled "now" with a thick line segment overlapping it, indicating an action happening right at this moment.*]
>
> * Don't disturb me now. I**'m working.**
> * We need an umbrella. It**'s raining.**
> * Hurry up! We**'re waiting.**

**Column 2:**
> **I have been doing**
> *present perfect continuous*
>
> [*LAYOUT: A right-pointing yellow arrow indicating an ongoing action, ending exactly at a vertical line labeled "now".*]
>
> * I**'ve been working** hard. Now I'm going to have a break.
> * The ground is wet. It**'s been raining.**
> * We**'ve been waiting** for an hour.
