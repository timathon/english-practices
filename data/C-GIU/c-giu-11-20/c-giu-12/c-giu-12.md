# Unit 12

## **for** and **since** | **when ... ?** and **how long ... ?**

[*LAYOUT: Section A block. Two parallel columns below, the left column explains 'for', the right column explains 'since'.*]
**A**
We use **for** and **since** to say how long something has been happening.

**We use for + a period of time:**

* We've been waiting **for two hours**.

[*VISUAL: A yellow arrow pointing to the right along a timeline, labeled "for two hours". Below the timeline, the start is labeled "two hours ago" and the end point is labeled "now".*]

| **for** |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| two hours | a long time | a week |
| 20 minutes | six months | ages |
| five days | 50 years | years |

* Sally has been working here **for six months**. (*not* since six months)
* I haven't seen Tom **for three days**.

**We use since + the start of a period:**

* We've been waiting **since 8 o'clock**.

[*VISUAL: A yellow-orange arrow pointing to the right along a timeline, labeled "since 8 o'clock". Below the timeline, the start is labeled "8 o'clock" and the end point is labeled "now".*]

| **since** |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 8 o'clock | April | lunchtime |
| Monday | 2001 | we arrived |
| 12 May | Christmas | I got up |

* Sally has been working here **since April**. (= from April until now)
* I haven't seen Tom **since Monday**.

---

[*LAYOUT: Section B block.*]
**B**
We often leave out **for** (but not usually in negative sentences):

* They've been married **for ten years**. *or* They've been married **ten years**.
* They **haven't had** a holiday **for** ten years. (you need **for**)

You can use **in** instead of **for** in negative sentences (**I haven't** ... etc.):

* They **haven't had** a holiday **in ten years**. (= **for** ten years)

We do *not* use **for** + **all** ... (**all day** / **all my life** etc.):

* I've lived here **all my life**. (*not* for all my life)

---

[*LAYOUT: Section C block.*]
**C**
Compare **when** ... ? (+ *past simple*) and **how long** ... ? (+ *present perfect*):

[*VISUAL: A window showing rain falling outside.*]
**A:** **When** did it start raining?
**B:** It started raining **an hour ago** / **at 1 o'clock**.

**A:** **How long** has it been raining?
**B:** It's been raining **for an hour** / **since 1 o'clock**.

[*VISUAL: A cartoon illustration of a man (Joe) in a yellow shirt and a woman (Kate) in a teal shirt looking at each other and smiling.*]
**A:** **When** did Joe and Kate first meet?
**B:** They first met { **a long time ago**. / **when they were at school**. }

**A:** **How long** have they known each other?
**B:** They've known each other { **for a long time**. / **since they were at school**. }

---

[*LAYOUT: Section D block.*]
**D**
We say:

| **it's** (= it **is**) <br>

<br> *or* **it's been** (= it **has been**) | a long time <br>

<br> six months (*etc.*) | since something happened |
| --- | --- | --- |


* **It's two years since** I last saw Joe. *or* **It's been two years since** ...
(= I **haven't seen** Joe for two years)
* **It's ages since** we went to the cinema. *or* **It's been ages since** ...
(= We **haven't been** to the cinema for ages)
* **How long is it since** Mrs Hill died? *or* **How long has it been since** ...
(= when did she die?)
