### Unit 8 Reading Comprehension: 2. The Small Mouse and the Cat

One day, a small mouse looked out of his room. He saw a hamburger. The small mouse said, "I'll get it and have it for breakfast." But he remembered his mum's words. "Always wait before you get a hamburger." So he waited in his room. Then he heard a quiet "Miaow" and he knew a cat was there. He said to himself, "I'm happy I listen to my mum and learn to wait." 

He waited for an hour, and then he heard a quiet "Woof, woof".  "A dog!" The small mouse thought. "If the dog is there, the cat won't be there." He ran out of his room but he met a cat. When the cat finished her breakfast, she said, "I'm happy I listen to my mum and learn a second language." 

Question 5: What did the small mouse see when he looked out of his room? Answer: A hamburger. 

Question 6: Why did the small mouse think the cat was out of his room? Answer: He heard the cat. 

Question 7: What did the cat have for his breakfast? Answer: A small mouse. 

Question 8: Which of the following is TRUE? Answer: The cat's mother was smart. 

### Unit 8 Reading Comprehension: 3. A Blind Man with a Lamp

Once upon a time, there lived a man by himself. He was blind, but he always took a lamp with him whenever he went out at night. One night when he came home after having dinner outside, he met a group of young men. They saw a blind man taking a lamp, so they made fun of him. 

One of them asked, "Hey, man! You are blind and can't see anything! Why do you take the lamp with you then?" The blind man said, "Yes, I am blind and I can't see anything, but the lamp is for the people like you who can see. They can see me coming and won't knock into me. That is why I take a lamp." The young men felt ashamed and said sorry to the man. 

The story tells us that we should think before judging others. Always be polite to others and learn to see things from others' point of view. 

Question 9: One night the man went out to? Answer: eat dinner. 

Question 10: After the man told the young men why he took a lamp, the young men? Answer: said sorry to him. 

Question 11: From the story, we know? Answer: we should learn to see things from others' point of view. 

Question 12: Which is the best title for the passage? Answer: A Blind Man with a Lamp. 

### Unit 8 Reading Comprehension: 4. Two Seeds

Once upon a time, there were two seeds. They were good friends. They often talked with each other when they were free. One day, they had nothing to do. They lay on the ground. They looked up at the sky and had a chat with each other. 

The first seed said, "I want to grow! Then I can enjoy the sun and the rain!" Later, it grew. It had a chance to enjoy the sun and the rain. 

The second seed said, "I am afraid of growing up. If I grow, I have to live in the soil. Little animals may come up to me and eat me. How terrible! If I become a beautiful flower, a child will come and pick me. It is much better for me to wait. It's a dangerous way to be alive." It did nothing and refused to grow. 

Spring came soon. A duck came up to the seeds. It ate the second seed and went away. See? We shouldn't act like the second seed. We should face our life bravely. 

Question 13: What did they do one day? Answer: They had a chat with each other. 

Question 14: The first seed wanted to grow because? Answer: it could enjoy the sun and the rain. 

Question 15: In the third paragraph, the underlined word "soil" means? Answer: 土壤. 

Question 16: From the passage, we should? Answer: face our life bravely. 

### Unit 8 Reading Comprehension: 5. The Little Tree

A man was walking in a forest. He was holding an axe in his hand. A little tree stopped him and said, "Please help me cut down those tall trees. Because of them, the sunlight cannot fall on me. And I have no room to grow at all." 

The man agreed and used his axe to cut down those tall trees. The little tree felt happy and said thanks to the man. But when the sun came out, the little tree lost water and became very thirsty. Later when a storm came, the little tree lost all its leaves and branches. 

"You shouldn't ask the man to do that. Those tall trees can protect you. So you don't worry about heat, wind and rain. You should cherish them. Now everything changes," a bird said. After hearing the words of that bird, the little tree felt regretful. From then on, the little tree made good friends with the trees around him. 

### Unit 8 Cloze Test: 1. The Wolf and the Fox

The wolf and the fox wanted to eat the rabbit, but it wasn't easy to catch him. One day the wolf said to the fox, "You go home and lie in bed. I will tell the rabbit you are dead. When he comes to look at you, you can jump up and catch him." "That's a good idea," said the fox. 

He went home right away. The wolf went to the rabbit's house and knocked at the door. "Who is it?" asked the rabbit. "It's the wolf. I've come to tell you that the fox is dead." Then the wolf went away. 

The rabbit went to the fox's house. He looked in through the window. He found the fox lying in bed and his eyes were closed. He said to himself, "Is the fox really dead? If he's not dead, he'll catch me when I go near him." So he said, "The wolf says the fox is dead. But this doesn't look like a dead fox. The mouth of a dead fox is always open." When the fox heard this, he thought, "I'll show him that I'm dead." So he opened his mouth. The rabbit knew that the fox wasn't dead, and he ran away as quickly as he could.

### Unit 8 Reading and Answering: 1. The Rose and the Cactus

One spring day, a rose blossomed in a forest. She greeted her neighbours, including a cactus, a pine and other plants. Then, she said to the cactus, "I'm the most beautiful flower here, but you are full of thorns and so ugly!" 

Day after day, the rose never stopped being unkind to the cactus. The cactus, however, never got sad or angry. Instead, he always calmly replied, "Every life has a purpose in existence." 

As summer came, the weather became hot and dry. The rose began to wither, but the cactus grew well. He had a wide root system and was good at storing water in his body. 

One day, the rose saw a bird make a small hole in the cactus with its beak. The thirsty bird drank the water inside the cactus through the hole. "It must hurt," said the rose. "Yes," the pine replied, "but the cactus doesn't want to see his bird friend suffer." 

The rose was so thirsty that she asked the cactus for water. He kindly agreed. The bird filled its beak with water and watered the rose's roots. The rose was ashamed of what she had said to the cactus in the past. From then on, she stopped judging others by their looks. 

Question 41: Why did the cactus manage to grow well during the hot and dry weather? Answer: Because of its wide root system and ability to store water in its body. 

Question 42: How did the thirsty bird drink water? Answer: The bird made a small hole in the cactus with its beak. 

Question 43: What lesson did the rose learn from her experience with the cactus? Answer: The rose learned that she shouldn't judge others by their looks.

### Unit 8 Written Expression: 1. The Story of Yu Gong

The Story of Yu Gong. Once there lived an old man called Yu Gong. Two high mountains stopped his family from walking to the other side. So Yu Gong decided to move the mountains. 

Although Yu Gong was old, he decided to move the mountains with his family. So Yu Gong started digging. At that time, a "clever" man called Zhi Sou laughed at him and told him that it was impossible to move such high mountains. But Yu Gong didn't listen to him and said that his sons and grandsons would continue to move the mountains after he died. 

Then a god was moved by Yu Gong so he sent two gods to help him move the mountains. The story reminds us that nothing is impossible as long as we work hard. We should not give up.
