### Grade 7 Final Test: 3. Reading Comprehension C

Wang Wei was a famous poet and painter from the Tang Dynasty. His poetry and painting styles influenced each other. "In Mojie's poems, you can see a painting; in his paintings, you can feel a poem," said the Song Dynasty poet Su Shi. 

Wang liked to paint people and natural scenes. He was the founder of the Southern School of Chinese Landscape Painting. This school is known for its strong brushstrokes and light ink washes. 

About 400 of Wang's poems still exist and 29 of them are included in the poetry collection Three Hundred Tang Poems. Wang was good at writing jueju poems. In the space of four lines, his jueju poems often describe peaceful mountains and rivers. The Deer Enclosure is a good example: 

In pathless hills no man's in sight, But I still hear echoing sound. In gloomy forest peeps no light, But Sunbeams slant on mossy ground. 

Wang Wei is remembered as one of the greatest poets and artists in Chinese history. His works continue to be read and loved to this day.

### Grade 7 Final Test: 4. Reading Comprehension D

People usually think that cats are not very friendly. Unlike dogs, cats play with people less than dogs. They like to stay alone for hours. However, a new study shows that cats like people just as much as dogs. 

Scientists at Oregon State University, the US, studied cats' relationships with their owners. They invited 117 cat owners to take a test. In the six-minute test, cat owners walked into a new room with their pets. After two minutes, the owners left the room, leaving the cats alone. When the owners came back two minutes later, the scientists looked at what the cats would do. About two-thirds of the cats came to greet the owners right away when they came back. The cats then went back to looking around the room, but still came back to their owners now and then. 

"These cats," the scientists said, "saw their owners as a source of security in a new place." In the same test, 58% of dogs acted just like the cats. "This idea that cats like to stay alone or don't really care about people isn't holding up," said Kristyn Vitale, a scientist who worked on the study. "The more we find out about cats, the more we see that they're social animals."

Question 14: Scientists made the test to find out? Answer: the relationship between cats and their owners. 

Question 15: The underlined phrase "holding up" in the last paragraph probably means? Answer: true. 

Question 16: What's the best title for the passage? Answer: Cats Are Our Friends.

### Grade 7 Final Test: 6. Hero Dog

However, the cat didn't like him, because she didn't want to live with another pet in the house. But the dog loved her and followed her all the time, trying to play with her. 

One day, a lynx caught the cat, and the lynx planned to have the cat for lunch. When the little dog saw this, he ran towards the lynx quickly, without any hesitation. The dog was much smaller than the dangerous animal. He did everything he could to save his friend. The children happened to see him, so after that they gave the dog a new name - "Hero Dog". 

From then on, the cat began to be friendly with the dog. She followed the little dog everywhere and even let the dog eat from her bowl. It was clear that the cat saw the little dog as a hero dog.
