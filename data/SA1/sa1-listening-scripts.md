## Welcome Unit
### Listening and Speaking
#### Conversation 1
Ms Li: Morning, Amy. How are you doing?
Amy: Very well, thank you. Ms Li, I want to be a designer in the future, so what courses do you think I should choose?
Ms Li: Oh, interesting! Well, first of all, I think an art course would be helpful to you. And there are some other courses. You should also take maths, physics and chemistry. They will help you understand design better.
Amy: OK, I see. What about languages? Should I take French?
Ms Li: That’s up to you. If you like it, go for it. It’s a useful language for designers.
Amy: Thanks a lot for your advice, Ms Li.
Ms Li: You’re welcome. Good luck with your choices!

#### Conversation 2
Li Ming: Hi, Max! Which courses did you pick?
Max: I chose biology, chemistry, maths and geography. I want to study medicine one day.
Li Ming: Wow, that’s a lot of science subjects. Do you like them?
Max: I love biology best. I’m really interested in how the human body works. What about you?
Li Ming: I’m taking history, art, English and Chinese. I hope to become a history teacher.
Max: That’s cool! History is full of amazing stories.

### Listening and Talking
Amy: Hi, Li Ming. Ready for the test? Do you have any study tips for me?
Li Ming: Not really. I just listen to the teacher and take notes.
Amy: Oh, group work is my favourite way to learn. I’m an outgoing person, so I like to discuss things. Talking to others helps me remember things.
Li Ming: I’m not very good at group work but I have other ways of remembering things. I like using flash cards. They are really effective.
Amy: Hmm... Notes and flash cards. I think you're a visual learner.
Li Ming: Yes, I guess so. I learn best when I look at pictures, so I make mind maps to organise my thoughts. I keep a learning diary, too. I write down what I've learnt and my thoughts. It helps me plan my learning and what I need to review for exams.
Amy: Ah, that's a good idea. I want to try that! My problem is I get distracted easily when I study alone.
Li Ming: Maybe you can study in the library. It’s quiet there.
Amy: Good idea, thanks!

## Unit 1 Teenage Life
### Listening and Speaking
#### Conversation 1
A: Hi, Jake! You look tired. What’s up?
Jake: I stayed up late last night to finish my report.
A: Why did you leave it so late?
Jake: I had basketball practice after school, then I helped my mum cook dinner. I had no free time until 9 pm.
A: That’s too busy. Do you have to do so many things every day?
Jake: Yeah. Homework, sports, housework... I barely have time to relax.
A: Maybe you should make a daily schedule to manage your time better.
Jake: I’ll try. Thanks for the suggestion.

#### Conversation 2
Sara: Hi, Tom. Are you joining the school volunteer club?
Tom: I’m thinking about it, but I’m worried it will take up too much study time.
Sara: It won’t. We only meet once a week after class. Last month we visited the old people’s home and read newspapers to them. It was really meaningful.
Tom: That sounds nice. What other activities do you have?
Sara: We also help clean the community park on weekends. You can make new friends there too.
Tom: OK, I’ll sign up with you tomorrow.

### Listening and Talking
Host: Welcome to Teen Talk! Today we’re talking about teenage stress. Let’s hear from our listeners.
Listener 1: I’m stressed about my exams. My parents expect me to get top grades, and I feel like I’ll let them down if I don’t do well.
Listener 2: My problem is after-school activities. I have piano, swimming and English classes every weekend. I never get time to hang out with my friends.
Listener 3: I feel stressed about making new friends in high school. I’m shy and I’m afraid no one will like me.
Host: Thank you for sharing. Here are two tips for you all. First, talk to your parents or teachers about your worries—they can understand and help you. Second, leave some free time every day to do what you love, like listening to music or walking outside. Small breaks can reduce stress a lot.

## Unit 2 Travelling Around
### Listening and Speaking
#### Conversation 1
Paul: Hi, Meilin! Where are you going this summer holiday?
Meilin: I’m travelling around Europe for two weeks with my aunt and uncle.
Paul: Europe? Oh, I’ve always wanted to go there.
Meilin: Yes, me, too. I’m so excited. I’m planning to visit France and Germany.
Paul: That’s wonderful! Do you have your passport and visa already?
Meilin: I got my passport last month, and the visa will come next week.
Paul: What places do you want to visit most?
Meilin: The Eiffel Tower in Paris and the Berlin Wall. I also want to try local food.
Paul: Don’t forget to take lots of photos!

#### Conversation 2
Booking Call
Woman: Skies Airlines. How can I help you?
Man: I’d like to buy a plane ticket from London to Paris.
Woman: Certainly, sir. On what date are you travelling?
Man: On the 23rd of December.
Woman: There are two flights that day: one in the morning at 9:30 and the other in the afternoon at 5:00. Which do you prefer?
Man: The 9:30 flight, please.
Woman: Would you like to travel business class or economy?
Man: I’d like to travel business class.
Woman: Would you like to book a return flight this time?
Man: No, thank you. I’m taking the train back.
Woman: May I have your name, sir?
Man: Robert Williams.
Woman: Thank you, Mr. Williams. I’ve made your booking. May I ask how you would like to pay?
Man: By credit card.
Woman: OK, wait just a second, please.

### Listening and Talking
M: Hi Jessica! I heard you love travelling. What’s your dream destination?
W: I’ve always dreamt of visiting New Zealand. The views there are wonderful and I’ve heard that people are pretty friendly. How about you?
M: That sounds amazing. Well, I’ve already been fortunate enough to visit a few countries. I’ve been to France, Italy, Spain, Thailand and Brazil. Each place had its own special experiences.
W: So when you travel, what do you always take with you?
M: My camera is a must. I love taking pictures of beautiful scenery. Besides, I take a travel guidebook to find local small shops and restaurants. A comfortable pair of walking shoes is also really important.
W: Which way of transport do you like best for travelling?
M: It depends. For short city trips, I walk or take buses to see local life. For long-distance trips between cities, trains are my favourite—you can sit back and watch the countryside outside the window.
W: That’s great advice. I’ll keep that in mind for my future trip.

## Unit 3 Sports and Fitness
### Listening and Speaking
#### Conversation 1
S=Shen Qi A=Amy
S: Hi, Amy! Did you hear that there’s an e-sports event this weekend?
A: No, I didn’t! What’re e-sports?
S: Well, e-sports are competitive games played on computers, like League of Legends.
A: Those aren’t real sports, are they?
S: Yes, they are official sports now. Millions of people watch competitions online. I’ve been waiting for this event for a long time. Would you like to come along?
A: Really? I’d love to! Where is it held?
S: It’s in the new city stadium. Why don’t you join us this Saturday afternoon?
A: Oh, sorry. I can’t. I have to meet my badminton coach for training.
S: Oh, that’s too bad. Maybe next time then.

#### Conversation 2
A=Adam J=Julie
A: Hey, Julie. What’re you doing this weekend? There is a Blue Paint Fun Run on Saturday afternoon.
J: What is a Blue Paint Run? Is it a marathon?
A: No, it’s not a marathon. It’s a fun 5km run for charity. You pay 20 dollars to join, and all the money goes to help poor kids get sports equipment.
J: Er… does everyone get covered in blue paint while running?
A: Exactly! It’s super fun and relaxing. Lots of students from our school are going.
J: Hmm, I’m not good at running, but it sounds interesting. Can I bring my friend Mia?
A: Of course! The more people, the better.

### Listening and Talking
Interviewer: Today we’re talking with Li Jia, a former high school track runner. Li Jia, how did sports change your life?
Li Jia: When I was 14, I was weak and shy. My PE teacher asked me to join the running team. At first I hated training—it was tiring and painful. But after half a year, I became stronger and more confident.
Interviewer: Did you face any difficulties?
Li Jia: Yes. Before a big race, I hurt my knee and wanted to give up. My teammates encouraged me every day, and we trained together slowly. I finally finished the race and got third place.
Interviewer: What advice would you give to students who don’t like sports?
Li Jia: Don’t think sports are only about winning. It’s about keeping healthy, making friends and learning to keep trying when things get hard. Find a sport you enjoy, even if it’s just walking or yoga.

## Unit 4 Natural Disasters
### Listening and Speaking
#### Conversation 1
M: Hello, emergency rescue centre. What’s your situation?
W: Help! There’s a small fire in my kitchen. The pan caught fire while I was cooking.
M: Stay calm. Turn off the gas first, and cover the pan with a lid to put out the fire. Don’t pour water on it!
W: OK, I’m doing that now. Is the fire truck coming?
M: We’ve sent a team to your address. They’ll arrive in 5 minutes. Stay outside the house and don’t go back in.
W: Thank you so much!

#### Conversation 2
Reporter: Mr. Wang, you lived through the flood last week. Can you tell us what happened?
Mr. Wang: It rained heavily for three days straight. The river rose fast and water flowed into our village. All the first floors of houses were flooded.
Reporter: How did everyone stay safe?
Mr. Wang: The village leaders called us to gather on the high hill early in the morning. We brought food and blankets with us. Volunteers brought drinking water and medicine later.
Reporter: Did you lose many things?
Mr. Wang: Most furniture and clothes got wet, but all people in our village are safe, which is the most important thing.

### Listening and Talking
Radio Host: This is your daily disaster safety guide. Today we learn how to prepare for an earthquake.
First: Before an earthquake, prepare an emergency bag with water, biscuits, a torch and a first-aid kit at home. Fix heavy furniture like bookshelves to the wall so they won’t fall over.
Second: When shaking starts, hide under a strong table or desk. Stay away from windows, tall buildings and power lines. Don’t rush outside immediately.
Third: After the earthquake, check for fires or gas leaks. Listen to official news on the radio—do not believe untrue online messages. Help old or disabled neighbours if you can.
Remember: Staying calm is the most important rule in any natural disaster.

## Unit 5 Languages Around the World
### Listening and Speaking
#### Speech Script (Main Listening Material)
One language sets you in a corridor for life. Two languages open every door along the way. —Frank Smith
Today I’m going to talk about world languages.
There are over 7,000 languages spoken around the world. Around 2.8 billion people speak the six official languages of the United Nations: Chinese, English, Spanish, French, Russian and Arabic.
Chinese has the largest number of native speakers, over one billion people. English is the most widely used global language for business, travel and science.
Why should we learn foreign languages? First, it helps us communicate with people from other countries and understand different cultures. Second, it brings more chances for jobs and study abroad. Third, learning a new language improves our brain and memory.
Some students think learning languages is hard, but small daily practice works better than long cramming. Watch foreign TV shows, talk with language partners or write short diaries every day. Every little step makes progress.

#### Conversation
M: Hi, Lingling. You can speak three languages—Chinese, English and Japanese. How do you learn them so well?
W: I watch Japanese cartoons every evening, and I write English diaries daily. I also join the school language exchange club to talk with foreign students.
M: I only speak Chinese. I want to learn Spanish, but I’m afraid of making mistakes.
W: Don’t worry! Mistakes are part of learning. Native speakers are always happy to help you correct them.
M: That’s encouraging. I’ll start learning Spanish next term.

### Listening and Talking
Panel Discussion
Host: Our topic today: Will English still be the global language in 50 years?
Speaker 1: I think yes. International trade, technology and pop culture all use English. Most universities teach courses in English, and most online information is written in English.
Speaker 2: I disagree. More and more countries are rising. Chinese, Spanish and Arabic speakers are growing fast. In the future, people may learn two main languages: their mother tongue plus one regional language, not only English.
Speaker 3: I believe multilingualism will become common. People will learn at least two foreign languages. No single language will fully control the whole world, but English will still be important for international communication.
Host: Thank you for your different opinions. Learning any foreign language will always be a valuable skill.
